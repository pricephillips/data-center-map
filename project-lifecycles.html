How to read the timeline
Each project row plots its known milestones left-to-right on a shared time axis. Click any dot to open the underlying event with its verified date and source.
Announced — the project became public (a filing, rezoning request, or company announcement).
Opposition — first and last recorded community opposition activity; the shaded bar between them is the span over which opposition was active.
Decision — advanced — a terminal approval (the project may proceed).
Decision — blocked — a terminal denial or withdrawal.
Why opposition can appear before ground is broken: a data center is opposed while it is still being proposed — during rezoning, permitting, or public hearings, well before any construction. Opposition is a response to the plan, and it is often what determines whether the project ever reaches groundbreaking. So on these timelines opposition sits between announcement and the decision that resolves it; it does not imply the project was ever built.
Dates are shown only when known. A decision dot appears only when the date has been manually verified and sourced. Month-only announcement dates are approximate (up to ~30 days).
